How to Find Cheap Flights
What Actually Works in 2026

The best booking windows by route, tools frequent flyers use, 5 myths to stop believing, and advanced strategies that save 20–40%

💡
Set a price alert, don't just search once

Google Flights' "Track Prices" feature notifies you when a fare drops. Set it up when you first identify a route — you're not committing to buy, just monitoring. Prices for the same route can swing by 20–30% over a few weeks in the 2–4 month window.

5 Myths That Are Costing You Money

Myth vs. Reality

Beliefs that frequent flyers have stopped acting on

Myth 1: Incognito mode shows cheaper prices. Reality: Most airlines don't track cookies to raise prices. Price increases during repeat searches happen because seats at lower fare classes sell out. Switch to incognito if you want — it won't help, but it won't hurt.

Myth 2: Book at 3am Tuesday for the cheapest price. Reality: No credible analysis supports a specific time of night. Prices update algorithmically based on demand and inventory, not a schedule.

Myth 3: Airline websites are always cheapest. Reality: OTAs sometimes have negotiated fares not available on the airline's own site, especially for international routes and package combinations. Always compare at least 3 sources.

Myth 4: Flash sales are always the best deal. Reality: Flash sales are genuinely good value if your travel dates align. If they don't, you may end up paying more to rearrange other parts of your trip than you saved on the ticket.

What actually works: Set price alerts early, compare multiple platforms, fly midweek, book 2–4 months out, and stay alert for genuine error fares (they still happen — specialist newsletters and Fare Alert services catch them fastest).

Advanced Strategies That Save 20–40%

1. Positioning Flights

Instead of flying BKK → London direct, fly BKK → Frankfurt, then a separate cheap short-haul to London. The combination is often €100–200 cheaper than a single through ticket. Works especially well when one hub has aggressively priced long-haul options.

2. Split Ticketing

Two separate one-way tickets instead of a return can save 30–40%. The risk: if your outbound flight is delayed and you miss your separately-booked return, the airline has no obligation to rebook you. Only works safely with generous connection times.

3. Open-Jaw Routing

Fly into one city, depart from another — for example, arrive in Paris, leave from Rome after travelling overland by train. This often costs less than a return ticket to either city and builds the overland trip into your itinerary.

🔐
Credit card points: the highest-value redemption

Airline miles earned through credit card spend are most valuable when redeemed for premium cabin long-haul flights. Business class BKK–London: roughly 50,000–80,000 miles depending on programme and availability. The same journey in cash costs €1,500–3,000+. The transfer ratio from general travel cards to airline miles varies — Amex Membership Rewards and Chase Ultimate Rewards typically offer the best transfer partners.

Before You Book: Final Checklist

✅ Pre-Booking Checklist
Run through this before clicking "confirm"
Compare at least 3 platformsAirline direct, one major OTA, and Google Flights. The cheapest source changes by route and date.
Check total price including all taxes and feesBudget carrier base fares can look 40% cheaper until you add seat selection, bags, and fees.
Check baggage allowance included in the fareSome fares include checked bags, others don't. Adding a bag after booking often costs double.
Read the change and cancellation policyFlexible fares cost more but can be worth it for trips more than 2 months away.
Check visa requirements for every country in your itineraryIncluding transit visa requirements — some nationalities need a visa even for airside transit.
Verify your name matches your passport exactlyName corrections after booking often cost as much as a new ticket on budget carriers.
✅
The bottom line

Finding cheap flights isn't about tricks — it's about timing, flexibility, and comparing the right sources. Book 2–4 months out, fly midweek, set price alerts, and check at least 3 platforms. The passengers who pay significantly less for the same seat are almost always doing one or more of these consistently.
