Online Check-in Tips
More Useful Than Most People Realise

When to check in, how to choose the best seat and why it's your most important pre-flight verification step

How Online Check-in Works

Online check-in is the process of confirming your travel in advance via the airline's website or app, without going to an airport counter. The result is a digital boarding pass that can be used directly at security and the gate.

Check-in Timeline: When to Act

48h
48 hours before departure
Check-in opens (some airlines)
Low-cost carriers like Ryanair and some Lufthansa routes open early. First to check in gets the widest seat selection — including exit rows and bulkheads.
24h
24 hours before departure
Check-in opens for most airlines
This is the standard window for most full-service carriers. The best moment to log in — set a reminder the day before your flight.
3h
3 hours before departure
Online check-in closes
After this point you must check in at the airport counter. Remaining seat selection is assigned automatically — usually the worst seats left.

6 Real Benefits Most Passengers Overlook

💺
Seat selection
The best seats are always taken first. The earlier you check in, the more choice you have.
🛡️
Overbooking protection
Passengers with confirmed boarding passes are generally protected before those who haven't checked in.
🔍
Verify your details
Name, flight number, date, airport — errors caught here cost nothing. At the airport they can cost a fortune.
⚡
Skip the check-in queue
Go straight to bag drop (if needed) or directly to security. No counter wait.
📊
See flight details early
Gate, departure time, actual flight number — all visible before you reach the airport.
📱
Digital boarding pass
Stored in Apple Wallet or Google Pay. No paper to lose, no printing required.
Real incident

Name on boarding pass didn't match passport — caught during online check-in, not at the airport

A passenger booked through an OTA where the name field auto-filled without a middle name field, creating a mismatch with the passport. During online check-in 24 hours before departure, the passenger noticed the discrepancy and called the airline immediately. The airline corrected it within an hour at no charge.

No cost, no stress. The same issue found at the airport could have resulted in a change fee of several hundred euros — or in a worst case, denied boarding for an international flight.
⚠️
Online check-in completed ≠ fully airport-ready

Even with a confirmed boarding pass, you still need to: drop checked bags (if applicable), pass passport control, clear security screening, show visas or additional documents, and clear arrival immigration at your destination. Check-in is the first step, not the only one.

🔐
Insider tips: what frequent travellers do differently
  • Set an alarm for the exact check-in opening time — if the flight is at 14:00 tomorrow, set an alarm for 14:01 today and log in the moment it opens
  • Emergency exit rows offer the most legroom and are often released at check-in — some airlines charge extra; worth checking
  • Save boarding pass in two places — wallet app AND a screenshot in your camera roll. Unreliable airport Wi-Fi or a dead battery shouldn't strand you
  • Check every letter of your name against your passport — including hyphens and accent marks
  • Some airports still require printed boarding passes — especially in parts of Asia and Africa. Check your destination airport before relying on a digital pass

Pre-Flight Checklist: Online Check-in

📋 Checklist: Online Check-in
Apply before every flight — prevents most check-in-related problems
Check in online the moment the window opensDon't wait until the day of travel. Set a reminder for 24h or 48h before departure.
Verify your name matches your passport exactlyEvery letter, including middle names, hyphens and accented characters. Before confirming.
Save boarding pass in two placesWallet app + photo screenshot in camera roll. Battery failure at the airport is a real scenario.
Check flight details on your boarding passFlight number, departure time, terminal and gate — verify these match your booking confirmation.
Even after online check-in, arrive at the airport on timeYou still need to pass security, border control and all other checkpoints.
Verify if your destination airport accepts digital boarding passesSome airports in certain regions require a printed pass. Check before leaving home.
✅
The bottom line

Online check-in takes 3 minutes and delivers six real benefits — from seat selection to overbooking protection to catching name mismatches before they become expensive problems. The passengers who skip it are usually the ones who end up in the middle seat in the last row, or standing at the airport trying to explain a detail that would have been trivial to fix from home.
