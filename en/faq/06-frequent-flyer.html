Frequent Flyer Programs
How to Earn Miles That Are Actually Worth Using

Compare 3 major alliances, Silver/Gold/Platinum benefits, and why the best miles are the ones you've already spent

Earning Miles That Actually Pay Off

Over 50% of frequent flyer cardholders let miles expire unused. Two reasons dominate: they don't know how to use them, and they wait until they have "enough" — but by then, the value per mile has already dropped. Understanding the basics before accumulating is what separates people who get free Business Class tickets from those with expiring balances.

The basic rule

Accumulate miles within one alliance. Miles earned on airline A can be redeemed for tickets on airline B within the same group. Splitting across multiple alliances dilutes your balance without reaching redemption thresholds in any of them.

🔐
The most overlooked benefit at Gold level

Gold status and above typically grants lounge access even when flying Economy. Priority Pass memberships cost €300–500 per year for similar access. If you fly 8+ times per year and can reach Gold, lounge access alone justifies the effort — without needing to fly Business Class.

Real scenario

Gold status reached through 10 Europe–Asia flights in 12 months

A consultant flying Bangkok–Frankfurt 10 times per year accumulated approximately 55,000 qualifying miles with Lufthansa Miles & More. Gold status was reached in month 9. Benefits: lounge access on all flights, priority boarding, 50% bonus on all subsequent miles earned.

Estimated annual value of Gold benefits: ~€420 in lounge access, €180 in baggage savings, €200 in miles bonus. Total additional value: ~€800/year from status that came from flights already being taken anyway.

Devaluation Risk: Why You Should Spend Before You Save

Airlines can reduce the value of miles without advance notice — this is permitted under their program terms. It has happened repeatedly:

  • United Airlines 2023: Required miles for some routes doubled overnight — 100% increase with no warning
  • British Airways 2022: Switched to dynamic pricing — miles required now fluctuate with cash ticket prices
  • Singapore Airlines 2023: Increased miles required for many Economy saver awards
⚠️
The fundamental rule of miles

Miles in your account are an unsecured liability that airlines can reduce at any time. The best mile is a mile you've already used. Saving for a "big redemption someday" while value erodes is the most common mistake in frequent flyer programs.

What happens when you wait

120,000 miles saved for Business Class — then the devaluation

A frequent flyer saved 120,000 miles over 3 years for a Business Class redemption priced at 110,000 miles. Six months before the planned redemption, the airline's update raised the requirement to 160,000 miles.

The redemption was no longer possible without earning an additional 40,000 miles. The planned Business Class ticket was worth ~€3,200 in cash. Three years of accumulation lost significant value in a single program change.

Best Redemption Strategy: Maximum Value Per Mile

The highest value per mile comes from Business and First Class long-haul redemptions — tickets worth €2,000–5,000 in cash but redeemable for the same miles that would only buy an Economy ticket worth €200–400 if used suboptimally.

💡
Partner redemptions often offer better value

Many programs allow redemptions on partner airlines at rates better than the program's own flights. Singapore KrisFlyer miles redeemed for Lufthansa Business Class can significantly outperform the same miles used on Singapore Airlines flights. Always check partner award rates first.

Checklist: Frequent Flyer Programs

✈️ Checklist: Miles & Status
Apply these principles to every program decision
Choose one alliance — accumulate in one programSplitting miles across programs dilutes everything. Concentrate in the alliance that covers your actual routes.
Spend miles within 12–18 months of earningDevaluation risk is real. Miles earned today may buy less next year. Redeem regularly rather than hoarding.
Target Business Class redemptions for maximum valueEconomy redemptions often return less than 1¢ per mile. Business Class long-haul returns 3–6¢ or more.
Check partner award rates before redeeming on home carrierAlliance partners sometimes offer better redemption rates than the program's own flights.
At Gold: track your lounge access usageIf you use lounges every trip, Gold is likely saving €300–500/year — worth maintaining intentionally.
Never let miles expireMost programs expire after 12–36 months of inactivity. Even a small hotel stay or partner purchase resets the clock.
✅
The bottom line

Frequent flyer programs reward people who fly regularly with the same airline. Choose based on your actual routes, accumulate consistently, and spend before the program changes the rules. The travellers who get the most from miles treat them as a perishable asset — not a savings account.
