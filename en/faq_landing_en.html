Introduction

Why You Need to Know This Before Booking

Flying in 2026 is no longer as simple as clicking "book" and showing up at the airport. Regulations, passenger rights, and security screening technology are evolving faster than most travellers realise — and the consequences of not keeping up are rarely just inconvenient. They cost real money.

A passport with "5 months and 29 days" remaining will get you denied at check-in, even though it technically hasn't expired. That's because the 6-month rule counts from your return date, not your departure. A connection that looks like it has enough time can disappear the moment you discover you need to clear immigration again. And the EU261 compensation you're legally entitled to — up to €600 per person — goes unclaimed by the vast majority of passengers who simply don't know it exists.

In 2026, several important changes have taken effect: Heathrow has lifted the 100ml liquid rule from February 2026 after completing a ยฃ1 billion CT scanner rollout across all terminals. Most other airports still enforce it. The ReFuelEU regulation now requires European airlines to blend 2% sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) — a cost that will eventually filter through to ticket prices. And ETIAS, the pre-registration system for visa-free travellers entering the Schengen zone, is expected to launch before the end of the year.

This guide is not a generic travel blog. It's built from over 10 years of real Ground Operations experience — handling passengers at check-in counters, managing special cases, and resolving airport emergencies. These are the things the person standing on the other side of the desk knows, but rarely has time to explain.

Whether you're a frequent flyer looking for an update, planning your first major trip, or a parent sending a child to study abroad — the 15 topics in this guide will help every flight go smoother, safer, and more cost-effectively.

€600

Maximum EU261 Compensation

A delay of over 3 hours on European routes longer than 3,500km entitles you to €600 per person — but only if you know how to claim it correctly.

6 Months

The Passport Validity Rule

Counted from your return date, not departure. One day short and you'll be turned away at check-in — regardless of how long you spent planning the trip.

2 Litres

Liquids at Heathrow

Heathrow lifted the 100ml rule from February 2026. But most airports still enforce it strictly — always check the rules at your specific airport before packing.

Checklist

15 Things to Check Before Every Flight

Bookmark this page and run through it before every trip — it covers every high-risk point where things typically go wrong.

Documents

Passport valid >6 months from your return date
At least 2 full blank pages in passport
Correct visa or e-visa for every country visited
Transit visa checked for every connection point
Name on ticket matches passport MRZ exactly

Connections

Connection time exceeds MCT by at least 30 minutes
Confirmed whether immigration clearance is needed
Watch the midnight trap — check date carefully
All flights on same PNR if booked as a sequence

Baggage

Liquids ≤100ml each (check destination airport rules)
Power bank in carry-on only · ≤160 Wh
Weight and size within airline allowance
Special items notified 48—72 hours before departure

Rights & Preparation

Online check-in completed 24—48 hours before flight
All boarding passes saved (needed for EU261 claims)
Travel insurance purchased with adequate medical cover
IATA airport code confirmed — not just the city name
