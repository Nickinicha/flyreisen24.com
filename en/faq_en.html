8 Essential Articles Every Traveler Should Know

Compiled from direct experience in the airline ticketing industry - knowledge you won't find on typical websites. Travel with confidence and security.

Documents and Preparation

2 Articles

Validity and Condition: Your passport must have more than 6 months validity from "return date" and must be in pristine condition - no tears or water stains (even tiny damage can result in denial)

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Note: Even if your passport has 7 months validity, but you travel for 30 days, you'll only have 6 months 1 day upon return - some countries won't accept this!

Diplomatic vs Regular Passports: Officials beware! Visa rights differ

  • Diplomatic/Official Passports (blue): For official duty only, some countries don't grant visa-free entry
  • Regular Passport (brown): For tourist travel, normal visa rights
  • Recommendation: Always bring brown passport for private travel

Proof of Onward Travel:

With one-way tickets, you need a "ticket to a third country" proving you won't overstay. Otherwise check-in will be blocked to avoid airline penalties.

üí° Pro Tip: Some countries accept bus or train tickets across the border as "Proof of Onward Travel" (cheaper than flight tickets)

IATA Timatic System: Airlines use the IATA Timatic database to determine your eligibility. What friends say doesn't mean the system will let you through.

Name Mismatch:

  • Name on passport ‚â† Name on ticket = No flight
  • 1 letter wrong = considered mismatch
  • Maiden vs married name requires supporting documents
  • Name correction: Contact agency within 24 hours

Cinderella Effect (Midnight Confusion): Midnight is a new day!

‚ö†Ô∏è Example: Flight departs January 10 at 00:30
You must arrive evening of January 9 (around 22:00)

Many passengers miss flights thinking midnight is still the previous day!

Minimum Connection Time (MCT): Airport minimum connection time is sacred

  • Typical MCT: 45-90 minutes (depends on airport)
  • International to International: 90-120 minutes
  • With customs clearance: Add 60-90 minutes
üí° Golden Rule: For separate bookings with less than MCT, all risk is yours (airline not liable)

Transit Visa:

Connecting through some countries requires a visa even without leaving the airport:

  • USA: Visa always required (even transit)
  • Australia: Visa always required
  • Canada: Some nationalities need eTA
  • Dubai/Doha/Istanbul: Usually not required (but always check)

Check-through Baggage: Bags checked through to final destination, except when clearing customs at first point of entry (e.g., USA, Australia)

Baggage and Security

2 Articles

100ml Rule (Liquid Rules):

  • All liquids (water, creams, gels, sprays) must be in containers max 100ml
  • In clear, resealable plastic bags max 1 liter (~20x20 cm)
  • 1 bag per person
  • 2024 Update: Europe strictly enforcing this rule again (Munich, Frankfurt)
üí° Exceptions: Breast milk, medications (with prescription), baby food = no 100ml limit (but must show to security)

Power Banks & Batteries:

  • Check-in forbidden: Power banks of any size must go in carry-on
  • Maximum size: 32,000 mAh (or 160 Wh)
  • Lithium batteries: Remove from devices, don't check
  • Spare batteries: In protective cases against short circuit
‚ö†Ô∏è Often Overlooked Prohibitions:
  • Lighter: 1 piece in baggage or on person, check-in forbidden
  • E-cigarettes: Check-in forbidden, use on plane forbidden
  • Smart Luggage: If battery non-removable = check-in forbidden
  • Sprays (check "Flammable" label): May be completely prohibited

Baggage Pooling:

Together in one booking = can combine weight

Example: You and a friend book together (2 people)

  • Total baggage: 20 kg √ó 2 = 40 kg
  • You use 25 kg, friend uses 15 kg = OK ‚úÖ
  • But: No single piece over 32 kg (worker safety)

Carry-on Mastery:

  • Standard size: 56 √ó 36 √ó 23 cm
  • Reality: Size varies by aircraft type
  • Small aircraft: Overhead bins very tight - soft-shell recommended (flexible)
  • Hard-shell 4 wheels: Better for checked baggage (durable, protects contents)

Weight Concept vs Piece Concept:

System Rule Example
Weight Concept Total weight, unlimited pieces 30 kg = 3 pieces √ó 10 kg ‚úÖ
Piece Concept Limited piece count 2 pieces (max 23 kg each)

Piece Concept Routes: Flights to Americas, Canada

32 kg Rule:

‚ö†Ô∏è No baggage piece can exceed 32 kg
Reason: Worker safety (Occupational Health & Safety)
If exceeded: Split into 2 pieces or pay oversized fee

Special Baggage (Sports Equipment):

1. Bicycles:

  • Deflate tires (explosion risk from pressure change)
  • Remove pedals
  • Turn handlebars parallel to frame
  • Pack in bike box or bag

2. Golf Equipment:

  • 1 equipment set = 1 bag (clubs + shoes + balls)
  • Some airlines free, others charge fee

3. Musical Instruments:

  • Small (guitar, violin): Can buy separate seat
  • Large (cello): 48 hrs advance notice required

4. Electric Wheelchair:

  • Declare battery Wh value 48 hrs in advance
  • Some battery types must be removed

5. Firearms:

  • License required
  • Weapon / ammunition packed separately
  • 48-72 hrs advance notice
