15 Essential Articles Every Traveler Should Know

Compiled from direct experience in the airline ticketing industry — knowledge you won't find on typical websites. From visas and transit rules to flight change rights, travel insurance, frequent flyer tactics, and student fare secrets. Travel with confidence and security.

Documents and Preparation

3 Articles
🛂 Validity and Condition: The 6-Month Rule With No Exceptions
  • Count from your "return date" only: Don't just check your passport's remaining validity on departure day. If you're on a long trip and your passport has less than 6 months left on return day (e.g., 5 months 29 days), you will be denied check-in immediately.
  • Condition is your entry pass: Immigration in Europe and Australia is extremely strict. If the visa pages have a water stain, a tiny tear even a millimeter long, or any peeling lamination, the document will be classified as "damaged" and you'll be sent back on the spot.
  • Why airlines act as the first barrier: Airlines cannot afford the risk. If you are denied entry at your destination, the airline is liable for a significant fine and must cover your return. Check-in agents are therefore required to stop you at the counter.
⚠️ Checklist: Critical Points to Verify (Must Read!)
  • Never collect souvenir stamps: Souvenir stamps from tourist attractions will invalidate your passport and prevent you from boarding any flight.
  • Blank pages rule: Many countries require at least 2 full blank pages. Even if your passport is years from expiry, a full stamp section means you cannot travel.
  • Moisture damage: A passport that has been wet and has warped or stiffened pages is a red flag immigration officials use to suspect document tampering.
📘 Blue vs Brown: Official Passport Holders, Don't Be Careless!

🔵 Diplomatic / Official Passport (Blue/Red Cover)

  • Issued for official government duties only.
  • The most dangerous misconception: Thinking the blue passport is "more powerful" in every situation. Visa exemptions for official passports often require travel to be for an official mission with an accompanying Official Note. Tourism is typically excluded.
  • The risk: Some Schengen states may not permit blue passport holders to enter as tourists — without a visa or mission confirmation, you will be detained immediately.

🟤 Regular Passport (Brown Cover)

  • This is the safest key for personal travel — clearest scenario for immigration officers worldwide.
  • Pro advice: Even when traveling for official duty, always carry your regular brown passport so that if you wish to extend your stay after completing your mission, you have the correct document — 100% legal and safe.
⚠️ Critical Warning: Never Swap Passports Mid-Trip
  • Using your blue passport to enter one country and then your brown passport to exit will confuse immigration systems and you may be flagged as an illegal immigrant.
  • Using an official passport for personal travel and any incident occurs will be immediately reported to your employer — using an official document outside its stated purpose is a breach of regulations.
✈️ Proof of Onward Travel & Name Mismatch

Holding a one-way ticket with no evidence of leaving your destination is one of the top reasons travelers are denied at the origin airport before they even board. Airlines share responsibility with immigration — if they allow you to board without proof of exit and immigration refuses you entry, the airline must pay to send you back and faces substantial fines.

💡 Solution for Open-Ended Travelers:
Many countries accept "cross-border bus or train tickets" that clearly show the travel date, time, and your name as evidence that you will not overstay.

Name Mismatch — Zero Tolerance: Name on passport ≠ name on ticket means you do not fly. First name and surname must match letter for letter. Contact the agency or airline within the first 24 hours of booking for free or minimal-fee corrections.

💡 The Most Important Tip: "The MRZ at the Bottom of Your Passport Is the Final Truth"

Open your passport to the photo page and look at the two rows of characters at the very bottom. The sequence in that strip is exactly what airline systems worldwide use to verify you. If you fill in your ticket exactly as shown in the MRZ, the chance of a mistake is zero.

⚠️ Critical: Visa-Free ≠ Entry Guarantee
Even with a visa-free arrangement, immigration officers retain the right to deny entry if they believe you intend to work illegally, overstay, or have insufficient funds. Always carry: proof of accommodation (hotel booking), return ticket, and evidence of sufficient funds (bank statement).
🌍 Key Visa Situations by Destination

🇺🇸 United States

  • ESTA (Electronic System for Travel Authorization): Required for Visa Waiver Program countries (most Western European passports, Japan, South Korea). Apply online at least 72 hours before travel. Cost: $21 USD.
  • B1/B2 Visa: Required for most other nationalities including Thailand, China, India. Apply at the US Embassy — processing can take weeks or months.
  • Important: ESTA approval does not guarantee entry — Customs and Border Protection (CBP) makes the final decision.

🇪🇺 Schengen Area (26 European Countries)

  • EES (Entry/Exit System): Launching in 2025, this biometric border system will track all non-EU visitors' entry and exit dates automatically.
  • ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorisation System): Expected to launch alongside EES, requiring visa-free nationals (including Thai, Australian, US passport holders) to pay €7 and pre-register online before visiting Schengen. Valid 3 years.
  • 90/180 Rule: Visa-free visitors may stay a maximum of 90 days in any 180-day period across the entire Schengen zone combined — not per country.

🇹🇭 Thailand (for International Visitors)

  • Visa Exemption: Over 60 nationalities enter visa-free for 30 days (some get 90 days).
  • Thailand e-Visa: Available for tourist, non-immigrant, and long-term visas through the official Thai e-Visa portal.
  • DTV (Destination Thailand Visa): Launched 2024 — a new 180-day visa designed for remote workers and digital nomads.
💡 Always Verify Through Official Sources
Visa rules change frequently. The most reliable check is IATA Timatic (see Article 01) or the official embassy website of your destination country. Never rely solely on information from travel blogs or social media.
🕛 The Cinderella Effect: Midnight Means a New Day!
  • The universal rule: The moment the clock strikes 00:00, that is the immediate start of a new calendar day.
⚠️ Classic Mistake:
Flight departs January 10 at 00:30

❌ Common misconception: Going to the airport on the evening of January 10 (the plane left nearly 24 hours ago!)

✅ Reality: Arrive at the airport on the evening of January 9 (around 21:00–22:00).

⚠️ The Golden Rule: Separate Tickets = Your Responsibility
If you book separate tickets, the airline bears zero responsibility if you miss your connection. You must buy a new ticket entirely at your own expense.
🛂 Transit Visa: Never Assume "Airport = No Visa"
  • 🇺🇸 United States: No "Transit Without Visa" for almost all nationalities. A C-1 transit visa is required.
  • 🇬🇧 United Kingdom: Many nationalities require an AVTV (Airside Transit Visa) even for airside transit.
  • 🇦🇺 Australia: Most nationalities must obtain a Transit Visa (Subclass 771).
  • 🇦🇪 Dubai/Doha/Istanbul: Generally no transit visa required if you hold a single itinerary ticket (Single PNR).
⚠️ "First Port of Entry" Rule — USA & Australia
Even if your ticket says bags are checked through to your final destination, you must collect your bags and carry them through customs at the first point where your feet touch their soil.

Example: Bangkok → Los Angeles → New York. Collect bags at Los Angeles, walk through customs, then re-check them.

Baggage and Security

2 Articles

Packing in 2026 is no longer about "one rule fits all airports." It's about understanding the technology at each specific airport.

📦 The New Era of CT Scanners
🆕 Major Update 2026: Liquid Rules Now Vary by Airport!

✅ Airports That Have Lifted the 100ml Rule (Up to 2 Liters Per Container)

  • 🇬🇧 United Kingdom: Heathrow (all terminals) officially lifted the rule from February 2026 following a £1 billion CT scanner investment. Also Birmingham, Bristol, Edinburgh, Gatwick.
  • 🇪🇺 Europe: Milan Linate and Malpensa (Italy) also allow liquids up to 2 liters per container.

❌ Airports Still Enforcing the 100ml Rule

  • 🇬🇧 Manchester, Luton, Stansted and other UK airports without CT scanners.
  • Most airports in Europe, Asia, and worldwide still using old screening systems.
⚠️ "What Passes on the Way Out May Be Confiscated on the Way Back!"
Always check the rules of your destination airport before packing large liquid containers.
🧴 Liquid Rules for Old-System Screening
  • Count by container, not contents: A bottle labeled 150ml with barely anything left = confiscated. Security only looks at the stated capacity on the packaging.
  • Definition of liquids: Includes gels, sprays, toothpaste, and semi-liquid foods (chili paste, jam).
  • 1-liter zip-lock bag: All liquids must fit in a single transparent re-sealable bag, sealed shut.
💡 Special Exempt Categories (No 100ml Limit)
  • Breast milk and baby food: May be brought in quantities necessary for the journey.
  • Prescription medications: Carry a doctor's note (in English) for faster security checks.
🔋 Power Banks & Batteries
  • Iron Rule — "No Checked Baggage": Power banks must be in hand luggage only.
  • Up to 20,000 mAh: Unlimited quantity (within reason)
  • 20,000–32,000 mAh: Maximum 2 units per person
  • Over 32,000 mAh (160 Wh): Forbidden in all circumstances
⚖️ Baggage Pooling
  • Same PNR is the key: Weight can only be combined when all passengers share the same booking reference. Separate bookings = no sharing.
  • Example: 20 kg each = 40 kg total. You use 25 kg, friend uses 15 kg = ✅ Pass
  • The 32 kg Rule (Health & Safety): No single bag may exceed 32 kg — no exceptions, ever.
⚖️ Weight Concept vs. Piece Concept

⚠️ The 32 kg Rule Cannot Be Broken
No single piece of baggage may exceed 32 kg regardless of system. This is for the safety of ground handling staff. Violations result in mandatory redistribution or very expensive oversize/overweight fees.
💡 Oversized Items (Sports & Special Equipment)
  1. Bicycles: Deflate tires, remove pedals, fold handlebars parallel to the frame.
  2. Musical Instruments: Small instruments can purchase a separate seat (Extra Seat); large instruments must be declared at least 48 hours in advance.
  3. Electric Wheelchairs: Must declare battery Wh value at least 48 hours in advance.
  4. Firearms: Valid license required; weapon and ammunition must be packed in separate containers; notify airline 48–72 hours in advance.
⚠️ Prohibited Items People Commonly Miss
  • Smart Luggage: Battery must be removable. If embedded and non-removable, most airlines refuse it as checked baggage.
  • Lighter: 1 piece per person in carry-on or on person. Absolutely forbidden in checked baggage.
  • E-Cigarettes: Forbidden in checked baggage. Forbidden to use or charge onboard.
  • Aerosol Sprays: Check for "Flammable" label — flammable aerosols may be banned entirely.

💡 Revalidation vs. Reissue
A Revalidation is a simple date change within the same fare class — cheap or free. A Reissue means the old ticket is cancelled and a new one is issued — significantly more expensive and subject to current pricing.
⚖️ EU Regulation 261/2004: Your Legal Shield

This is one of the most powerful pieces of consumer protection legislation in the world. It applies to:

  • All flights departing from an EU airport (any airline)
  • All flights arriving at an EU airport operated by an EU carrier

⚠️ When Compensation Does NOT Apply
Airlines are exempt if the disruption was caused by "extraordinary circumstances" beyond their control — severe weather, ATC strikes, political unrest, security threats, or pandemics. Technical failures caused by the aircraft's own maintenance issues are typically NOT considered extraordinary circumstances.
🍽️ Right to Care: Always Applies

Regardless of whether compensation applies, if your flight is delayed by 2+ hours (short-haul), 3+ hours (medium-haul), or 4+ hours (long-haul), the airline must provide:

  • Meals and refreshments proportional to the waiting time
  • Hotel accommodation if an overnight stay becomes necessary
  • Two free phone calls, emails, or faxes
💡 How to Claim EU261 Compensation
  1. Keep all boarding passes, booking confirmations, and delay/cancellation notices
  2. Submit a written claim to the airline directly (use registered email — keep copies)
  3. If denied, escalate to your country's National Enforcement Body (NEB) or use claim service platforms
  4. You have up to 6 years to make a claim in many EU countries
🎟️ Ticket Validity and the Void Window
  • Most standard economy tickets: Valid for 1 year from the date of issue.
  • Promotional/discounted fares: May be valid only for fixed dates — no changes permitted.
  • The 24-hour Void Window (USA rule): Under US DOT rules, airlines selling tickets for US-origin/destination flights must allow free cancellation within 24 hours of booking, if the flight is at least 7 days away. Many international airlines voluntarily honor this globally.

❌ What Travel Insurance Does NOT Cover (Read Before Buying!)
  • Pre-existing medical conditions — unless specifically declared and covered with a premium. Always disclose everything.
  • Extreme sports and high-risk activities — skiing, scuba diving, motorbike riding in many countries requires specific add-on coverage.
  • Traveling against government advice — if your government has issued a "Do Not Travel" advisory for your destination, most standard policies will not pay out.
  • Self-inflicted injuries and alcohol or drug-related incidents are universally excluded.
  • Epidemic/pandemic exclusions — many policies added COVID-19 exclusion clauses. Read carefully.
⚠️ The Fine Print Trap
The most common insurance claim denials happen because travelers didn't read the exclusion clauses. Pay particular attention to: the definition of "pre-existing condition," the minimum delay trigger for compensation, documentation requirements (you must report lost baggage to the airline within 7 days under the Montreal Convention), and claim submission deadlines.
💰 When Is Travel Insurance Worth It?
💡 Buy travel insurance if any of these apply:
  • Traveling to the USA, Canada, or Australia — medical costs can be catastrophic
  • High prepaid, non-refundable trip cost (cruises, safari packages, premium flights)
  • Pre-existing health conditions or older age group travelers
  • Adventure activities or remote destinations with limited medical facilities
  • Traveling during typhoon/hurricane/monsoon seasons in risk-prone destinations
⚖️ The Montreal Convention: Your Base Protection Even Without Insurance

The Montreal Convention (MC99) is an international treaty that applies automatically to all international flights. It provides:

  • Baggage loss: Up to approximately SDR 1,288 (~$1,700 USD) per passenger
  • Flight delay damages: Up to SDR 5,346 (~$7,000 USD) for proven consequential losses
  • Death or injury on board: Automatic liability up to SDR 128,821

To claim under the Montreal Convention, you must file a written complaint with the airline within 7 days of receiving damaged baggage, 21 days for delayed baggage, or 2 years for other claims.

⚠️ The Birthday Trap: Infant Becomes a Child Mid-Trip
If your child turns 2 years old on the return journey date, they require their own seat and must be charged the child fare (75–100% of adult fare). Call the airline's Call Center to issue a mixed ticket (Split Passenger Type).
📍 Directionality: Why Origin Country Affects Price
  • The principle: Airlines set prices based on the cost of living and purchasing power of the origin country. Flying Bangkok → London may be cheaper than London → Bangkok due to fierce competition from Middle Eastern carriers in the Thai market.
  • Currency advantage: Booking in the local currency of a country whose currency is currently weak may save an additional 3–5%.
  • VPN Caution: While VPN-based price searching is widely discussed online, be aware that booking through mismatched IP addresses can sometimes trigger fraud checks or booking problems.
🤝 Codeshare Flights: Who Is Flying You?
  • Check-in: Always go to the counter of the Operating Carrier (the airline actually flying the aircraft), not the airline whose code is on your ticket.
  • Baggage rules: Follow the Operating Carrier's rules, not the airline you paid.
  • Miles: You can earn miles according to the partner program conditions — check both airlines' websites.
🎟️ Stopover & Open-Jaw: Multiple Cities in One Ticket
  • Stopover: Staying more than 24 hours at a transit city. Emirates, Qatar, and Turkish Airlines often offer Free Stopover Programs including complimentary hotels or city tours.
  • Open-Jaw: Fly into Paris then travel overland and fly home from Rome. Same price as a return, but you cover more ground.
  • Double Open-Jaw: Change both the inbound and outbound cities — e.g., BKK→Paris / Rome→Singapore. Can save significantly on both cost and travel time.

🌟 Alliance Structure: Why It Matters
  • Star Alliance: Thai Airways, Lufthansa, Singapore Airlines, United, ANA — earn and redeem miles across all members
  • Oneworld: British Airways, Qatar Airways, Cathay Pacific, Finnair — known for excellent business class redemptions
  • SkyTeam: Korean Air, Air France, KLM, Delta — strong transatlantic coverage
💡 The Most Important Rule: Miles Expire!
Most programs expire miles after 12–36 months of account inactivity. Keep your account active by earning or redeeming even small amounts — a single hotel booking or credit card transaction resets the clock in most programs.
🎫 Redemption Strategies: Getting Maximum Value
  • Sweet spots: Business and First Class redemptions typically offer the best value per mile — often 4–6 cents per mile vs 1–2 cents for economy cash-equivalent.
  • Partner redemptions: Redeeming Avios (British Airways Avios) on partner airlines like Japan Airlines or Iberia can unlock significant value for short-haul flights.
  • Avoid: Miles for merchandise or low-value gift cards — almost always the worst redemption rate.
  • Book early for premium cabins: Award space in Business Class is released 11–12 months in advance and disappears quickly.

⚠️ The Status Run Trap
Some travelers fly purely to maintain status (known as "mileage runs"), buying cheap tickets on long routes just before the year ends. Calculate carefully — the cost of status-qualifying miles through actual travel often exceeds the value of benefits received, especially for casual travelers.
🎓 What Are Student Fares?

Student fares are specially negotiated rates available to full-time students, typically aged 12–32, enrolled in accredited institutions. They are not always the cheapest base price — but the total value package is usually far superior to standard tickets.

🔍 Where to Find Student Fares
  • Direct from Airlines: Lufthansa, British Airways, Emirates, Singapore Airlines, Cathay Pacific, Thai Airways all offer dedicated student programs. Apply online with proof of enrollment.
  • StudentUniverse: Global booking platform exclusively for students and youth. Verifies student status and unlocks exclusive rates not available publicly.
  • STA Travel-affiliated agencies: Specialised agents with access to consolidated student rates from multiple airlines.
  • ISIC (International Student Identity Card): The globally recognized student ID accepted by airlines and travel partners in 130+ countries. Annual fee ~$28 USD — often pays for itself with a single discount.
🖥️ OSI and SSR Codes: Insider Knowledge

Student fares are typically booked with special codes in the airline's reservation system that unlock additional services:

  • SSR DOCS: Student documentation code — airlines may request proof of enrollment at check-in. Always carry a current enrollment certificate or student ID.
  • OSI (Other Service Information): A note in the PNR from the booking agent confirming student fare eligibility. Ensures no dispute at check-in about your baggage allowance.
⚠️ Always Carry Proof at Check-In
Agents have the right to verify student status at check-in, particularly for extra baggage allowance. If you cannot produce a valid student ID or enrollment certificate, you may be charged full standard excess baggage fees at airport rates — which are significantly more expensive than pre-purchased excess baggage.
🧳 Maximizing the Baggage Benefit
  • Compare total value, not just ticket price: A student fare at $50 more than a budget carrier may include 35 kg baggage vs the budget carrier's zero — saving $80–$150 in excess baggage fees.
  • Book early for popular routes: Student fare inventory (the number of available seats at the student rate) is limited — popular routes like Bangkok–London or Bangkok–Frankfurt sell out months in advance.
  • Combine with frequent flyer programs: Student fares often earn full miles. Combine enrollment in a frequent flyer program from day one of university — four years of study travel adds up significantly.

✈️ Charter Flights: The Unscheduled Heroes

A charter flight is an aircraft hired for a specific purpose, outside of regular scheduled airline service. Types include:

  • Tour Operator Charters: Tour companies hire entire aircraft for package holiday groups — common for European beach destinations.
  • Evacuation Flights: Government-arranged mass evacuation during crises (civil unrest, pandemics). Seats are typically allocated by embassy registration — sign up with your embassy's citizen registration system before traveling to high-risk destinations.
  • Ferry Flights: Aircraft being repositioned with no passengers. Occasionally sold at deeply discounted rates through specialist brokers.
  • Air Ambulance: Medical evacuation flights for critically ill patients — covered by comprehensive travel insurance.
  • VIP / Private Charter: Individuals or companies hiring aircraft for direct routing without scheduled service constraints.
💡 Travel Tip for Risky Destinations
Always register with your home country's embassy citizen registration service (e.g., UK FCDO LOCATE, US STEP program) before traveling to politically unstable or disaster-prone areas. If evacuation flights are organized, registered citizens are prioritized.
🪑 Overbooking: Legal, Common, and Manageable

Airlines routinely sell more seats than physically available on aircraft — this is legal and based on sophisticated statistical modeling of no-show rates. Historically, the industry no-show rate averages 5–15% per flight.

What Happens If You're Bumped?

  • Voluntary bumping: Airlines first ask for volunteers who willingly give up their seat in exchange for compensation (typically travel vouchers, miles, or cash) plus rebooking on the next available flight.
  • Involuntary bumping: If insufficient volunteers come forward, the airline selects passengers to be denied boarding — typically those who checked in last or bought the cheapest tickets.

⚠️ Protect Yourself from Overbooking
  • Always check in online as early as possible (24–48 hours before) and confirm your seat.
  • Paid seat selection significantly reduces your chance of being bumped.
  • Elite status holders are almost never involuntarily bumped — another reason to build status.
  • If bumped involuntarily, do not accept vouchers without knowing their cash equivalent first.
✈️ 3-Letter Airport Codes: Always Double-Check
  • Problematic codes: BKK (Bangkok) ≠ BBK (Botswana). SYD (Sydney, Australia) ≠ SDY (Sidney, Montana, USA).
  • Same city, multiple airports: London (LON) has LHR, LGW, STN, LCY. Switching between terminals/airports independently requires 5–6 hours minimum.
  • IATA vs. ICAO: 3-letter codes (BKK) for passengers; 4-letter codes (VTBS) for pilots and ATC.
🔌 Power Plugs and Voltage
  • Main plug types: Type A/B (USA/Japan/Thailand), Type C (Europe), Type G (UK/Singapore/Hong Kong)
  • Voltage: The 110V system in the USA and Japan can damage Thai and European appliances rated at 220V. Always carry a Universal Adapter.
  • Best buy: A travel adapter with USB-C ports built in — handles both plug conversion and charging modern devices.
💺 Seat Configuration: How to Choose Wisely
  • Layout matters: 2-4-2 is best for couples — both window seats, no middle seat trapped. 3-4-3 is more cramped.
  • Pitch & Width: Check the seat pitch (legroom distance) and seat width on tools like SeatGuru.com before selecting — these vary greatly between airlines and aircraft types.
  • The IFE Box Factor: Avoid seats with an In-Flight Entertainment (IFE) box taking up under-seat floor space — common in window seats on certain aircraft.
  • Emergency exit rows: Extra legroom but seats typically do not recline. Tray tables are in the armrest (narrower). Not suitable for travelers needing armrest support to sleep.
📲 Online Check-in: The Smart Traveler's First Move
  • Check in online 24–48 hours in advance to select your preferred seat first.
  • Reduces overbooking risk: Checked-in passengers are last to be bumped — a checked-in confirmation is strong evidence of your seat.
  • Save your boarding pass offline in case of poor airport connectivity. Screenshots also work as a backup.
⚖️ Two Legal Frameworks Every Traveler Should Know

Two separate legal frameworks protect you on international flights. Understanding both is essential for knowing when and how to claim:

📋 Step-by-Step: How to Make a Successful Claim
  1. Document everything immediately: Take photos of damaged baggage, keep all boarding passes, print or screenshot delay notifications from the airline.
  2. File a Property Irregularity Report (PIR) at the airport baggage desk before leaving the arrivals hall. This is required for any Montreal Convention claim.
  3. Send written complaint within deadlines:
    • Damaged baggage: within 7 days
    • Delayed baggage: within 21 days
    • EU261 compensation: within up to 6 years (varies by country)
  4. Address the claim correctly: Send to the airline's official Customer Relations department — not the check-in desk or call center.
  5. Escalate if needed: National Enforcement Bodies (NEBs) in each EU country handle EU261 complaints for free. Non-EU travelers can use Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) schemes.
💡 Claim Services: Worth It or Not?
Companies like AirHelp, ClaimCompass, and Flightright handle EU261 claims for a commission of 25–35% of the compensation. Worth it if: you don't want the hassle, the airline is unresponsive, or you need court representation. Not worth it for straightforward cases where the airline accepts the claim directly.
⚠️ What Voids Your Claim
  • Missing filing deadlines (especially the 7-day damaged baggage rule)
  • Accepting a voucher without confirming it doesn't waive your cash compensation rights
  • Not having a boarding pass or booking confirmation to prove you were a passenger
  • The disruption being caused by extraordinary circumstances (see Article 06)
🚀 SAF — From Legislation to Reality (2025–2026)
🆕 Major Update: ReFuelEU Aviation Is Now Law!
  • 🇪🇺 European Union: ReFuelEU Aviation entered into force from early 2025, starting at 2% SAF and scaling up to 70% by 2050.
  • 🇨🇭 Switzerland: Joined the ReFuelEU framework from 1 January 2026.
  • 🇹🇭 Thailand: Voluntary targets of 1% SAF in 2026, scaling to 8% by 2036.
⚠️ The Production Gap: IATA estimates SAF production will reach only about 1.9 million tonnes in 2025 — approximately 0.8% of total aviation fuel. Far short of the 10% target for 2030. Flight prices may rise partially during this transition.
  • What is SAF? Sustainable Aviation Fuel is produced from renewable feedstocks — organic waste, used cooking oil, energy crops. Reduces carbon emissions by over 80% vs conventional jet fuel.
  • Supersonic Return — Boom Overture: Aims to solve the sonic boom problem that grounded Concorde, enabling supersonic flight over land. Bangkok to London in under 8 hours.
  • Hydrogen-Powered Aircraft: Airbus is testing hydrogen propulsion systems as part of a long-term roadmap to net-zero aviation by 2050.
  • Clear Air Turbulence (CAT): Future aircraft will use Lidar (laser-based) technology to detect and automatically avoid invisible atmospheric turbulence.
