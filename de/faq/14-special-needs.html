Special Passengers & Children
Airline Rules & What to Request in Advance

Unaccompanied minors, wheelchair assistance, pets, and medical passengers — advance planning makes the difference between a smooth trip and a denial at the gate

The Most Important Rule for Special Passengers

Airlines accommodate special passengers well — but almost every service requires advance notice. Walking up to check-in with a child travelling alone, a mobility issue, or a pet carrier and expecting everything to work without prior arrangement is the most common cause of disrupted travel for this group. The earlier you request, the smoother the experience.

Unaccompanied Minor (UM): Children Travelling Alone

AgeStandard rule
Under 5Cannot travel alone on any airline
5–11 yearsMandatory UM service (fee applies)
12–14 yearsUM or Young Adult service depending on airline
15+ yearsMost airlines treat as adult passengers

What UM service includes: staff handover from parent/guardian at check-in, escort through security, priority boarding, seat near crew, handover only to the named person at destination (ID required). Not included: the other adult waiting at destination showing up without the pre-registered ID — the child will not be released.

Approximate UM fees per sector: most European carriers €40–80, major Asian carriers $30–60, US carriers $100–150. Fees are per sector, not per trip.

⚠️
The receiving adult must have valid ID matching the booking

UM service takes the handover protocol seriously. If the person collecting the child at the destination doesn't match the registered details exactly, the airline will not release the child — even if they're a close family member. Register the correct person when booking and do not change it informally.

Wheelchair and Mobility Assistance: IATA Codes

IATA CodeMeaningWhat to expect
WCHRCan walk, cannot climb stairsWheelchair on apron/jetbridge only
WCHSLimited mobility, can manage short distancesWheelchair from check-in to seat
WCHCFully non-mobileFull assistance every step, aisle chair to seat
WCBDDry cell battery wheelchairPermitted in cabin (battery stays with chair)
WCBWWet cell battery wheelchairSpecial cargo hold requirements

Request the correct code when booking. Confirm 24–48 hours before departure. Wheelchair assistance is free on all airlines and at all airports under international aviation law.

Pets in the Cabin and Cargo

Pet typeIn-cabinCargo holdKey conditions
Small dog/catAirline-dependentYesMax ~8kg including carrier (in-cabin)
Large dog/catNoYesIATA-approved crate required
Exotic/exotic speciesNoCase-by-caseCITES documentation required
Guide/assistance dogAll airlines, free—Accreditation documentation required

Documents required for pets: health certificate from a vet (issued within 10 days of travel — not earlier), full vaccination records including rabies, microchip (mandatory for EU entry), and a health/import certificate for the destination country. Some countries have strict quarantine rules regardless of documentation.

🚨
Australia, New Zealand, and UK: strict biosecurity requirements

These countries have some of the world's strictest pet import rules. Australia and New Zealand require months of advance preparation, rabies titration tests, and approved quarantine stays. Research the destination country's specific rules at least 6 months in advance for these destinations.

Medical Passengers and Pregnancy

MEDA (Medical Clearance): required when a condition might be affected by the flight environment or could affect other passengers. The airline's medical team reviews the case; a doctor completes the INCAD form (Incapacitated Passenger Handling Advice). Request at least 72 hours before departure — earlier for complex conditions.

Pregnancy stageStatus
Up to 28 weeksUsually no restrictions
28–36 weeksMedical certificate required
Over 36 weeksMost airlines refuse travel

Rules vary by airline and route length. Always get written confirmation from your specific carrier before booking travel after 28 weeks.

Real situation — UM service breakdown at destination

Amsterdam — child not released; wrong adult at collection

A 9-year-old was booked on UM service from Bangkok to Amsterdam. The grandfather who booked the service arranged for an aunt to collect the child at AMS instead. The aunt was a close family member but was not the registered contact in the booking.

The child was held in the airline's care at AMS for 4 hours while the situation was resolved. The airline would not release the child to anyone not named in the original booking, regardless of family relationship. The registered contact (grandfather in Bangkok) had to provide written authorisation remotely before any handover was made.
The correct approach: update the registered contact in the booking before travel if plans change. Airlines allow this but require it to be done through official channels before departure — not by phone call at the destination.

Special Passenger Checklist

✈️ Before You Book & Before You Fly
For every special assistance category — request early, confirm again closer to departure
Request all special services at booking, not at the airportUM, wheelchair, pet transport — all require advance reservation. Last-minute requests are frequently denied or can't be accommodated.
Confirm 24–48 hours before departureCall the airline to confirm the request is still on the booking. Special service annotations occasionally drop off reservations after changes.
UM: verify the receiving adult's exact details are in the bookingName and ID must match exactly. Any change must be done officially before departure, not informally by phone at destination.
Pet health certificate: get it 7–9 days before travel, not earlier10-day window closes fast. Too early and it expires before travel; too late and there's no time for vet appointment issues.
Pregnancy over 28 weeks: get a letter before bookingDon't buy the ticket and then seek confirmation — get written clearance from the specific airline first.
Medical conditions: request MEDA at least 72 hours outEarlier for complex cases. Denial at the gate with no pre-clearance leaves no options — it's too late to arrange.
